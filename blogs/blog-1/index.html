RESEARCH

The research mainly included surveys, questionnaire, brainstorming without any direction, help and interviews.

The brainstorming session was the first thought we had related to the topic. It was a tool for generating as much as solutions or ideas as possible for the topic. Later we defined the problem , layed out the context and definitions , included the right people , set the agenda and then started holding the sessions.

SECONDARY RESEARCH.

  • gist of problems
  • changes
  • improvement factors
  • classification of planters
  • design statements

The topic was now finalized as PLANTERS

The detailed research began and we now found our PROBLEM AREAS, which were:

● REPOTTING : After a certain stage, a plant starts growing to an extent where the normal size planter cannot hold its strength and so depending on each plant, the repotting session happens.

● WATERING SYSTEM : People generally do not use a watering can to water their plants ; also each plant acquires a limited amount of water. The most preferred technique is spray bottle, sprinkling water, water bottles or a pipe.

● CLIMATE CHANGES : Climate changes depend on the area, city, country. But the required protection methods are not taken. When in Summers , the plant requires some shadow which is totally neglected. Other seasons face the problems related to the atmosphere there.

● CLEANLINESS : The major issue faced in indoor gardening system is the cleanliness issue as most of the planters leave the water stains on the tiles. Also terracotta planter is a big task to handle but is eventually best for the plant.

● DRAINAGE SYSTEM : The indoor balconies usually don’t have a proper drainage system, they mostly call people to clean and maintain it. Also least attention is paid to this system.

● SPACE : Because the balconies have a limited amount of space, the planters are very compact and do not have enough space for breathing. Also the person does not have enough space to walk in and take care of the plant.

● PLANTERS USED : There are various types of planters used such as terracotta,plastic, glazed ceramic, etc. Each of it’s pros and cons. Depends totally on the user on what planter he prefers.

INITIAL DESIGN BRIEF.

“ TO REDESIGN A PLANTER TO OPTIMIZE ITS FUNCTIONING.”

Field Visit

Why field visit.?

  • Studying such a vast topic was not possible just with internet and books; and to study deeply about our project, we decided to visit some houses, nurseries, gardens etc.

With the help of these visits, we could shortlist our pros and cons for the particular project.

OBSERVATION:

  • Balconies have a limited amount of space.
  • Not everyone is interested in indoor gardening but is just done for the aesthetic purpose of the house.
  • The drainage system is a big problem.
  • Cleanliness is an issue because of the marks that the pots leave and also water.
  • Existing planters create problems for relocating, repotting, cleaning, and so on.
  • There needs to be devised an alternate arrangement of the planters along with
    alternate material for the planters.
  • A convenient system for watering the plants along with a method to make the planters mobile is required.

The observation also varied from a house to a nursury. This helped us take decisions in a very open-minded space and mind.

REDESIGNED BRIEF

“ TO DESIGN A CONVENIENT PLANTER FOR MIDDLE AGE PEOPLE HAVING INDOOR GARDEN IN THEIR BALCONIES. ”

PERSONNA

CONCEPTUALIZING

IDEATION OF OUR PRODUCT

* Since our designated space was a balcony; Effective Space Management was a prime focus hence stacking it one over the other seemed practical. * Spinning on this concept we came across terrace farming which ensures least wastage of water since the it’s passed on from on surface to another and by the the time the soil seeps it in. Ensures constant water levels. * As it the bases of tray will be slightly inclined and Porus in the front allow the flow of excess water to pass down in the next tray. * The last tray has a pipe connected to it which opens up directly in the drainage hole. * It helps as a inbuilt drainage system for well kept balconies. * It has wheels attached to it for internal balcony movement; Due to which the planters don’t have to moved individually as season requirements. And can me moved together. * The cloth hanger is optional but it helps the cloth dry faster. Win win situation. * From our research we found out that the plants tend to grow better when there is moisture in the environment they are in. * And since our space is balcony it serves its purposes. as well as it benefits the clothes vice-versa by absorbing the moisture content from them.

FINAL PRODUCT

BILL OF COST AND SUGGESTED MATERIAL

For the tray since we require strong, long lasting and waterproof material we chose:

  • PP plastic(poly propylene) cost 100/kg powder. It’s cost effective. If we can play with the budget then PC(poly carbonate).
  • For the fabrication/ structure we thought of Extrusion PVC pipes of Schedule40/80 which costs as 100/10ft. Thickness 1/2in.
  • We chose this material since the product demanded a non rusty, strong,light weight material.

SCOPE OF IMPROVEMENT

  • TO ENABLE CHANGES IN THE PLANTERS SETTINGS AS PER USERS AESTHETIC
  • TO ADJUST THE HEIGHTS OF THE TRAY AS PER REQUIREMENT.
  • TO ALSO CHANGE THE MOBILITY HANGER BACKWORDS LIKE A TROLLEY SO THAT THE USER HAS NO BACK PROBLEMS.
  • INCLUDING LOCK SYSTEMS IN THE TYRES.
